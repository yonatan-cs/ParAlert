# PRODUCT.md — SafeNet

## Register
product

## Product purpose
SafeNet alerts a parent when their child is involved in cyberbullying (WhatsApp-first).
Each alert carries the offending message + surrounding context, a toxicity score, who-did-what,
and — the core value — an empathetic, concrete recommendation for what the parent should do.
The system covers three angles: my child as victim, as aggressor, or as bystander.

## Users
Hebrew-speaking parents in Israel. Not necessarily technical. A parent who installs this is a
parent who will act. They read alerts on their phone, often in the evening, often worried.
They are not security analysts; they need clarity and guidance, not data.

## The physical scene
A parent glances at their phone after dinner, anxious, asking "is my kid okay, and what do I do?"
The interface must answer both calmly, in seconds, without inducing panic.

## Tone
Calm, supportive, trustworthy, empathetic. Serious but never alarmist. The product reduces panic,
it does not manufacture it. It guides, it does not judge the parent or the child.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)
- Surveillance/"spyware" coldness — this is care, not espionage.
- Alarmist red-everything that spikes a worried parent's stress.
- Generic SaaS admin dashboard (KPI tiles, hero metric, identical card grids).
- Childish or playful styling — the subject is heavy.
- Clinical/enterprise security-tool look.

## Strategic principles
1. Empathy first — the action recommendation is the hero, not the score.
2. Calm under stress — a worried parent must parse it instantly.
3. The 3-angle framing (victim / aggressor / bystander) is the product's spine.
4. Severity is communicated, not screamed. Color encodes, it doesn't assault.
